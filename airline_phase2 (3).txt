# Phase II: Business Process Modeling
## Airline Reservation and Flight Management System

### Business Process Overview

This document describes the core business processes of the Airline Reservation and Flight Management System, focusing on Management Information System (MIS) relevance.

---

## Scope Definition

**Primary Business Process:** End-to-end flight reservation and operational management

**MIS Relevance:** 
- Order processing (booking reservations)
- Resource management (aircraft and crew allocation)
- Revenue tracking and financial reporting
- Safety compliance monitoring
- Real-time operational decision support

**Objectives:**
1. Automate passenger booking and payment processing
2. Ensure safe crew scheduling with qualification validation
3. Prevent scheduling of maintenance-overdue aircraft
4. Generate analytics for route profitability and capacity optimization
5. Maintain comprehensive audit trails for compliance

**Expected Outcomes:**
- Reduced booking errors by 90%
- 100% crew safety compliance
- Zero flights with overdue maintenance
- Real-time revenue and occupancy insights

---

## Key Entities and Roles

### 1. **Passengers (External Users)**
- **Role:** Book flights, make payments, receive confirmations
- **Responsibilities:** Provide accurate information, complete payments
- **Data Generated:** Personal details, booking preferences, payment records

### 2. **Reservation Agents (Internal Users)**
- **Role:** Process bookings, handle customer inquiries, manage seat assignments
- **Responsibilities:** Verify availability, confirm reservations, process payments
- **Data Generated:** Reservation records, payment transactions

### 3. **Flight Operations Team**
- **Role:** Schedule flights, assign crews, monitor flight status
- **Responsibilities:** Ensure crew qualifications, validate aircraft readiness
- **Data Generated:** Flight schedules, crew assignments, status updates

### 4. **Maintenance Department**
- **Role:** Track aircraft maintenance, update service records
- **Responsibilities:** Log maintenance activities, flag overdue services
- **Data Generated:** Maintenance logs, safety alerts

### 5. **Management/Analytics Team**
- **Role:** Monitor KPIs, analyze revenue, optimize routes
- **Responsibilities:** Review reports, make strategic decisions
- **Data Generated:** Business intelligence reports, trend analysis

### 6. **Database System (Automated)**
- **Role:** Enforce business rules, trigger alerts, log audits
- **Responsibilities:** Validate data integrity, prevent violations, generate notifications

## Swimlane Process Flow Description

### **Lane 1: Passenger**
- Initiates booking request
- Selects flight and seat
- Completes payment
- Receives confirmation

### **Lane 2: Reservation System (Automated)**
- Checks flight availability
- Validates seat availability
- Processes payment
- Generates reservation record
- Sends confirmation

### **Lane 3: Flight Operations**
- Reviews flight schedule
- Assigns qualified crew members
- Validates aircraft maintenance status
- Updates flight status (Scheduled → Boarding → Departed)

### **Lane 4: Maintenance System (Automated)**
- Monitors aircraft maintenance dates
- Triggers alerts for overdue maintenance
- Blocks non-compliant aircraft from scheduling
- Logs all maintenance activities

### **Lane 5: Management/BI Layer**
- Receives aggregated data
- Analyzes revenue per flight
- Reviews occupancy rates
- Identifies profitable routes

---

## Key Decision Points

1. **Is the flight available?**
   - YES → Check seat availability
   - NO → Suggest alternative flights

2. **Is the seat available?**
   - YES → Proceed to payment
   - NO → Offer other seat options

3. **Is payment successful?**
   - YES → Confirm reservation
   - NO → Retry or cancel

4. **Is crew member qualified?**
   - YES → Assign to flight
   - NO → Select alternate crew

5. **Is aircraft maintenance current?**
   - YES → Allow scheduling
   - NO → Block flight, send alert
